The Challenge

I Found Traders Drowning in Noise Instead of Acting on Structure

Retail traders in stock and crypto markets share a common pattern: they watch dozens of overlapping indicators, read conflicting signals, and make emotional decisions under time pressure. The problem is not lack of data—it is lack of structured, actionable signal architecture.

Most available indicators solve one narrow problem (trend, momentum, volatility) but never combine direct and indirect signals into a decision framework. Traders end up building ad-hoc setups that break across market conditions.

Core Scenario
A crypto swing trader watches 6 separate indicators on a BTC/USDT chart. RSI says overbought, MACD shows momentum, but the trend is still bullish. Without a system that synthesizes these signals, every trade is a judgment call—and judgment degrades under stress.

Deliverable 01

I Mapped the Signal Landscape Before Writing a Single Line of Code

I started with deep research into trading signal categories: trend-following (MA, EMA, SMMA), momentum (RSI, MACD, Stochastic), volatility (ATR, Bollinger Bands), and volume-based (Accumulation/Distribution). The goal was not to build another indicator—but to understand which signal combinations create reliable decision frameworks.

This research phase produced a signal taxonomy that became the foundation for every indicator. Instead of building tools in isolation, I designed a system where each indicator covers a specific layer—and the X Strategy combines them all.

Deliverables
  • Signal taxonomy across 4 layers: trend, momentum, volatility, volume
  • Competitive audit of 20+ existing TradingView community indicators
  • 3 user personas with distinct trading styles and signal needs
  • Jobs-to-be-done framework: “Help me see structure in market noise so I can act decisively”
Deliverable 02

I Designed the Buy/Sell Bot to Replace Judgment With Visual Signals

The first indicator I shipped was a Buy/Sell signal bot that automates the most common trading decision: when to enter and when to exit. Using AI, I researched signal combinations and generated Pine Script code that synthesizes multiple trend-change signals into clear visual markers.

The bot overlays directly on the price chart—green triangles for buy signals, red for sell. No separate panel, no interpretation needed—the signal is the interface.

Design Decision
I chose chart-overlay signals instead of a separate oscillator panel. Traders scan price action first—signals that live on the chart reduce cognitive load and decision latency.
Deliverables
  • Pine Script indicator with automated BUY/SELL signal generation
  • Strategy variant built on top of the indicator for backtesting
  • AI-assisted data research to identify optimal signal parameters
  • Published indicator description and usage documentation
Deliverable 03

I Built the ATR Stop Loss Indicator to Make Risk Management Visual

Most traders know they need stop losses but struggle to place them correctly. I created an Average True Range indicator that converts abstract volatility data into concrete stop loss lines directly on the chart.

The indicator calculates ATR-based stop loss ranges using configurable smoothing methods (SMA, EMA, WMA) so traders can see exactly where their risk boundary sits relative to current price action.

Why ATR Matters
Fixed-percentage stop losses ignore market volatility. ATR-based stops adapt to current conditions—wider in volatile markets, tighter in calm ones. This is risk management as information design: making the invisible (volatility) visible and actionable.
Deliverables
  • Pine Script ATR Stop Loss indicator with configurable smoothing (SMA, EMA, WMA)
  • Visual stop loss line overlaid directly on price chart
  • AI-assisted research into optimal ATR periods for different asset classes
  • Published documentation with setup guidance for traders
Deliverable 04

I Designed X Strategy as a Unified Decision System, Not Another Indicator

X Strategy is the most comprehensive tool in the suite. It combines multiple indicators into a single unified strategy that shows current trends, ranges, important levels, entry/exit signals, and recommendations—all on one chart.

Instead of asking traders to interpret 6 separate indicators, X Strategy synthesizes direct and indirect signals into a coherent decision layer. This is information architecture applied to financial data.

Architecture Decision
I rejected building one more standalone indicator. Instead, I designed X Strategy as a meta-system—a strategy container that orchestrates sub-indicators into a single testable, backtestable decision framework.
Deliverables
  • Comprehensive Pine Script strategy combining trend, momentum, volatility, and volume signals
  • Multi-indicator code architecture with internal signal orchestration
  • Strategy testing guidelines for backtesting across market conditions
  • AI-assisted parameter optimization and edge-case testing

Lessons

What Building Trading Tools Taught Me About Information Design

01
Information architecture applies to any data domain, not just screens
Organizing trading signals into a 4-layer taxonomy was the same skill as organizing a product navigation. The medium changes; the methodology is identical.
02
The best decision tools reduce choices, not add options
Traders don't need more indicators. They need fewer, better-combined signals. X Strategy's value is synthesis—removing the interpretation burden from the user.
03
AI accelerates domain expertise when you bring the right questions
AI generated Pine Script code and tested parameters, but the value came from knowing which signal combinations to explore. Domain framing is the human layer; code generation is the AI layer.
